## Summary  

The paper proposes a **time‑management framework** that combines prioritization techniques (including the **Eisenhower Matrix**), scheduling, delegation and technology tools to improve productivity in dynamic workplaces  (Callula et al., 2024). Case studies show measurable gains in project completion time, turnaround time, and project visibility after applying the framework  (Callula et al., 2024).

## Critique/Limitations  

- Evidence is drawn from **case studies**, not randomized controlled trials; causality between the Eisenhower Matrix and outcomes is suggestive rather than conclusive  (Callula et al., 2024).  
- Multiple techniques (Eisenhower Matrix, Pareto, time‑blocking, tech tools) were used together, so the **specific contribution** of the Eisenhower Matrix is hard to isolate  (Callula et al., 2024).  
- Results focus on short‑ to medium‑term project metrics; long‑term effects on habits, decision quality, or burnout are not evaluated  (Callula et al., 2024).
